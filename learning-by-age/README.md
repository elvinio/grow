# Learning by Age

Learning looks different at every stage of life — and so does the support that works best. This section maps the science of learning to real contexts in Singapore, from the early primary years through the adult learner returning to study in their fifties.

Each file covers how the brain develops at that stage, what the research says about effective learning strategies, and practical guidance for parents, educators, and learners themselves.

## What's inside

| File | Who it's for |
|------|-------------|
| [Primary School (Ages 7–12)](./primary-school-7-12.md) | Parents and teachers of P1–P6 students navigating foundational habits, PSLE pressure, and the balance between drilling and deep learning |
| [Secondary School (Ages 13–16)](./secondary-school-13-16.md) | Parents, teachers, and secondary students managing the adolescent brain, O/N-level prep, identity, and burnout |
| [JC / Poly / ITE (Ages 17–19)](./jc-poly-ite-17-19.md) | Students and parents across the three post-secondary pathways — A-levels, applied diplomas, and vocational mastery |
| [Adult Learners (Ages 40+)](./adult-40-plus.md) | Working adults, mid-career switchers, and lifelong learners navigating SkillsFuture, part-time study, and time-scarce lives |

All entries are grounded in learning science and written with the Singapore education landscape in mind.
