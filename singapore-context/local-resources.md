# Learning Resources in Singapore: Free and Low-Cost Options for Students and Adults

Singapore has an unusually rich ecosystem of free and subsidised learning resources — for students from primary school through to working adults. Many of these are underused because families default to paid tuition or enrichment programmes. This guide maps out what's available, how to access it, and what each resource does well.

---

## Free Government Resources

### MOE Student Learning Space (SLS)

The Student Learning Space is MOE's official digital learning platform, available to all students in MOE schools. Access is via a school-issued account at **learning.moe.edu.sg**.

**What's available:**
- Curriculum-aligned videos, interactive activities, and assessments for all subjects from Primary to Secondary level
- Self-directed learning modules that students can work through at their own pace
- Teacher-assigned lessons and quizzes used in class

**How to use it well:**
- Use subject-specific search to find content aligned to specific syllabus topics
- The practice questions are calibrated to exam-style formats — more useful than random worksheets
- Parents cannot log in directly, but students can access it at home using their school credentials

SLS is genuinely good and significantly underused. Many students only encounter it when a teacher assigns something. Proactive use — especially for revision of specific weak topics — can substitute for paid tuition in many cases.

### NLB: National Library Board

Singapore's public library system is more than just books. NLB offers a broad range of learning resources through its branches and the **NLB Mobile app**.

**Key resources:**
- **eResources portal (nlb.gov.sg/main/services/eResources):** Access to thousands of eBooks, audiobooks, magazines, and academic databases — free with a library membership. Notable offerings include PressReader (international newspapers), JSTOR, and Lynda.com equivalents.
- **Overdrive / Libby:** Borrow eBooks and audiobooks from NLB's digital collection on your phone or tablet
- **Physical study spaces:** Most NLB branches have quiet study areas with free Wi-Fi. Branches like the National Library (Victoria Street), Jurong Regional Library, and Tampines Regional Library have extensive seating and reference collections
- **NLB programmes:** Libraries regularly run reading programmes for children, coding workshops, author talks, and learning events — many free

A library card is free for Singapore citizens and PRs. Get one. The digital resources alone are worth far more than the cost of many paid subscriptions.

### SkillsFuture Credit

**SkillsFuture** is a national programme to support lifelong learning for Singapore residents aged 25 and above. Every eligible Singaporean receives a credit balance that can be used to offset course fees.

**What it covers:**
- Thousands of courses listed on the SkillsFuture Course Directory (myskillsfuture.gov.sg)
- Subjects range from digital skills, business management, and leadership to language learning, culinary arts, and creative writing
- Courses from MOE TAFE, polytechnics, private training providers, and international platforms like Coursera and LinkedIn Learning

**How to claim:**
- Log in with Singpass at myskillsfuture.gov.sg
- Search for a course, check eligibility, and apply credit at checkout
- Employers can also top up SkillsFuture Enterprise Credits for company-sponsored training

For adult learners looking to upskill or change careers, SkillsFuture is one of the most practical resources available in Singapore. The key is identifying what skills you actually want to develop, then finding accredited courses that match.

### IMDA Digital for Life

The Infocomm Media Development Authority's **Digital for Life** initiative provides free digital literacy resources for Singaporeans, particularly seniors, lower-income families, and those new to technology.

**Resources include:**
- Digital literacy workshops run through community centres and libraries
- The Digital Access for All programme provides device and connectivity support for lower-income households
- Online learning modules for basic digital skills: internet safety, using smartphone apps, basic productivity tools

For families with seniors who are trying to get comfortable with technology, or for community members who are less digitally confident, these programmes are a practical entry point.

## Free Online Resources Accessible in Singapore

### Khan Academy (khanacademy.org)

Khan Academy is one of the best free learning resources on the internet, full stop. It covers Mathematics from primary level through university calculus and statistics, Sciences, Computing, Economics, History, and more.

- Video explanations are clear and paced well
- Adaptive practice tracks what you know and targets gaps
- The Maths sequence aligns closely enough to the Singapore curriculum to be genuinely useful for PSLE through JC students
- Completely free, no registration required to watch; free account enables progress tracking

Primary school students can use it for mathematics reinforcement. Secondary students will find it useful for E-Math and A-Math concepts. JC students can use it for H2 Math calculus and statistics content.

### CS50 (cs50.harvard.edu)

Harvard University's Introduction to Computer Science course — CS50 — is available entirely free online through edX. It is widely regarded as one of the best introductory computing courses in the world.

- Covers programming fundamentals, data structures, algorithms, web development, and Python
- Problem sets are challenging and genuinely engaging
- Certificate of completion available for a fee; the course itself is free to audit
- Suitable for motivated Secondary and JC students, polytechnic students, and adults

For anyone wanting to learn to code from scratch — or understand computing at a deeper level — CS50 is an excellent starting point.

### Coursera (Audit Mode)

Coursera hosts courses from universities including Johns Hopkins, University of Michigan, Google, and IBM. Most courses can be **audited for free** — you access the video lectures and readings without paying for the certificate.

Good for: adult learners exploring new fields, JC and poly students wanting depth beyond the curriculum, professionals building specific skills in data science, marketing, finance, or UX design.

To audit: enrol in a course, look for the "Audit" option (sometimes under "Financial Aid" or "Try for free"), and proceed without payment.

### YouTube Learning Channels

YouTube has become a serious educational resource. Useful channels for Singapore students and adults:

- **Kurzgesagt:** Complex science and philosophy topics explained clearly with excellent animation — good for building intellectual curiosity in older children and adults
- **3Blue1Brown:** Mathematics explained with visual intuition — covers linear algebra, calculus, statistics. Essential viewing for A-Math and H2 Math students who want to understand, not just compute
- **CrashCourse:** Series covering Biology, Chemistry, Physics, History, Economics, Psychology — well-paced and suitable from Secondary level upwards
- **Professor Leonard:** University-level calculus and statistics, highly recommended for JC H2 Math students who want thorough worked examples

## Coding and Technology Education

### Code in the Community

A collaboration between Tinkercademy, Google, and other partners, **Code in the Community** offers free coding classes for primary and secondary school students from lower-income families. Courses cover Scratch, Python, and web development.

Applications are open periodically; check codeinthe.community for the current cycle. For eligible families, this is an excellent alternative to paid coding enrichment programmes.

### Tinkercademy

Singapore-based Tinkercademy (tinkercademy.com) specialises in coding education for schools and individuals. They offer both paid enrichment programmes and curriculum support. Their blog and YouTube content include free tutorials for beginners using Python and micro:bit.

### Replit (replit.com)

Replit is a browser-based coding environment — no installation required. Students can write and run code in Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Java, and many other languages directly in the browser.

Useful for: students who want to practise coding without setting up a development environment, school projects that require collaboration, and beginners who want to experiment without commitment.

## Community and Peer Learning

### People's Association (PA) Community Centre Classes

Community centres across Singapore run affordable classes in an enormous range of subjects: languages (including Mandarin, Japanese, Korean), arts and crafts, music, dance, fitness, and basic computing. Fees are typically low — often $20–$80 for a multi-week course.

For adults who want structured learning in a social environment, CC classes are a frequently overlooked option. Find your nearest CC and its programme schedule at pa.gov.sg.

### Public Library Study Groups and Programmes

NLB branches actively support group learning. Many branches run structured reading programmes, homework help sessions for primary students, and community study initiatives. The library environment itself supports focused study, and the social presence of other learners helps with motivation.

### Reddit r/SGExams

The subreddit **r/SGExams** is an active online community of Singapore students sharing study resources, exam tips, moral support, and subject-specific advice. It is particularly active during PSLE, O-level, and A-level seasons.

The community posts resource links, subject guides, past year paper compilations, and study technique discussions. Quality varies, but the collective knowledge is substantial and the tone is generally supportive.

### LinkedIn Learning (via NLB)

NLB members can access **LinkedIn Learning** (formerly Lynda.com) for free through the library's eResources portal. LinkedIn Learning offers thousands of courses in software skills, business, creative topics, and professional development.

This is particularly valuable for adult learners and job seekers who want professional certificates in tools like Microsoft Excel, Adobe Creative Suite, project management software, or data analysis platforms.

## Subject-Specific Tools

| Tool | Best For | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| **Anki** (apps.ankiweb.net) | Flashcard-based memorisation for vocabulary, definitions, formulae | Free (desktop); small fee for iOS app |
| **Desmos** (desmos.com) | Graphing calculator for mathematics — excellent for visualising functions | Free |
| **Pleco** | Chinese-English dictionary with character recognition and flashcard decks — invaluable for Mother Tongue | Free (with paid add-ons) |
| **Wolfram Alpha** | Step-by-step mathematics solutions, science data, unit conversions | Free (basic); subscription for full steps |
| **Notion / Obsidian** | Personal note-taking and knowledge organisation for older students and adults | Free tiers available |

Anki in particular is backed by strong evidence. The spaced repetition algorithm it uses is one of the most effective tools for long-term retention of facts — useful for Science terminology, Chinese vocabulary, History dates, and language learning at any age.

## A Note on Using These Resources

Having access to good resources is not the same as using them well. The most effective learners are not those with the most tools, but those who engage with a small number of resources consistently and actively.

Pick one or two resources that match your current learning goal, use them with intention, and give them enough time to work. A student who uses Khan Academy for 20 minutes daily for a month will likely progress more than one who downloads five apps, watches a few videos, and moves on.

The resources listed here are free or low-cost. The investment required is time, attention, and the willingness to sit with difficulty long enough to understand it.
